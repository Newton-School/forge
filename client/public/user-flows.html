Forge User Flows & Ownership Guide

Who does what, who assigns it, and how every screen works

A plain-language map of the Profile Building Drive platform. It answers the core questions directly — who raises blockers, who gives reviews, who sets milestones, tasks and deliverables — and then walks every item in each role's sidebar: what it is, why it exists, what it does, how it works, and who owns it.

01The big picture — a chain of accountability

Each level sets the frame for the level below it and reviews the work coming up from it. Nobody works in isolation: the system carries a student's progress upward and guidance downward.

Admin / LCC
Build the frame: domains, teams, phases, gates, review cadences, escalation thresholds, rubrics.
Admin LCC
Teacher (Faculty)
Owns a domain. Approves gates, signs off milestones, does the final weekly review (L4).
Teacher
Mentor (Student Mentor)
Leads one team. Defines milestones & deliverables, assigns tasks, reviews mentees (L2/L3), manages blockers.
Mentor
Mentee (Student)
Does the work. Posts updates (L1) incl. blockers, completes tasks, submits deliverables, rates the mentor.
Mentee
One important rule: the Student Mentor is also the team lead — there is no separate “Team Lead” role. The Mentor both mentors the students and owns team delivery (the board, issues/PRs, blockers, team deliverables).

02Quick answers (who does what)

The questions people actually ask, answered in one line each.

QuestionAnswer
Who raises blockers?The Mentee raises a blocker inside their 2-day update (the “Blocker” field), or the Mentor logs one on their behalf. It surfaces to the Mentor first.
Who resolves / escalates a blocker?The Mentor works it. If it stays unresolved (default 3 days) it auto-escalates to the Teacher; still stuck → LCC.
Who gives reviews?It is a chain: Mentee writes L1 (self-update) → Mentor writes L2 (status) & L3 (weekly, Sat) → Teacher writes L4 (weekly decision, Sun).
Who sets milestones?The Mentor breaks the project into milestones — inside the phases/gates that Admin/LCC configured. The Teacher signs them off (Gate 2).
Who creates & assigns tasks?The Mentor creates tasks and assigns them to a Mentee. The Mentee updates progress.
Who sets deliverables?Admin defines the deliverable types (e.g. report, demo video). The Mentor requires them per milestone; the Mentee/team submits; the Mentor approves/rejects.
Who approves the project idea?The Teacher at the faculty gate (Approve / Revise & Resubmit / Reject).
Who raises a concern?Anyone. It creates a ticket emailed to LCC (CC the organizing team) and is resolved by LCC (or the Teacher within a domain).
Who rates the mentor (360°)?The Mentee — “was the mentor available? was the feedback useful?” — feeding mentor accountability.

04The review loop (L1 → L2 → L3 → L4)

The heartbeat of the drive. A 2-day pulse from the student rolls up into a weekly evaluation that the mentor and teacher complete in turn.

L1 · Mentee Update
Every 2 days, ~3 min. Four fields: Worked On · Learning · Blocker · Next Goal. Append-only.
Mentee writes
L2 · Mentor Status
Every 2 days the mentor sets each mentee’s status 🟢🟡🔴 + an action note. Auto-flags pre-fill (no updates 3/5 days, repeated blocker).
Mentor writes
L3 · Weekly Review (Sat)
Mentor writes Progress Summary, Strength, Improvement Area & sets On Track / At Risk / Needs Discussion.
Mentor writes
L4 · Teacher Review (Sun)
Teacher reads L3 and records the decision: Continue / Monitor / Schedule Discussion (+ notes).
Teacher writes
Why it’s built this way: the student supplies raw signal (L1), the mentor turns it into an assessment (L2/L3), and the teacher makes the call (L4). The platform auto-flags silence and stale blockers so attention lands where it’s needed — no one has to hunt a spreadsheet.

05Work setup (phase → milestone → task → deliverable)

How a unit of work comes into being and who owns each step.

Phases & Gates
The drive structure (Explore → Propose → Build → Showcase) and approval gates are configured once.
Admin LCC
Project approved
Team/mentee proposes a project; the teacher approves it at the faculty gate.
Mentee → Teacher
Milestones defined
Mentor breaks the project into milestones with acceptance criteria & due dates; teacher signs off (Gate 2).
Mentor → Teacher
Tasks assigned
Mentor creates tasks under a milestone and assigns each to a mentee.
Mentor → Mentee
Deliverables submitted
Mentee/team submits the required deliverable (type set by Admin); mentor approves or rejects.
Mentee → Mentor

06Blockers & concerns lifecycle

Two related but distinct things. A blocker is “I’m stuck on the work.” A concern is a formal ticket about a person/process/technical/other issue.

Mentee Blocker path

Raised
Mentee types it in the “Blocker” field of a 2-day update (or the mentor logs it).
Seen by Mentor
Appears on the mentor’s dashboard; “Blocker Streak” counts repeats.
Worked / resolved
Mentor guides; resolution recorded. Same blocker 3+ updates → “Repeated Blocker” flag.
Escalated
Unresolved 3 days → Teacher; still stuck → LCC. May be promoted to a Concern.

Any role Concern (ticket) path

Raised
Anyone clicks “Raise Concern”: category, severity, description.
Ticket + email
System creates a ticket and emails LCC, CC the organizing team. SLA timer starts.
Triaged
LCC acknowledges → assigns → in progress. Teacher handles domain-scoped ones.
Resolved & closed
Resolution note added; reporter notified; auto-closes after a window (can reopen).

07Mentee sidebar — every item explained

Mentee is self-scoped: they see only their own records, plus their team’s shared milestones. They are the doers — most objects here are created by others and acted on by the mentee.

Dashboard/mentee
Why
A single “where do I stand / what’s next” home so the student never has to guess.
Does
Shows identity (mentor, teacher, team, squad), overall progress, milestones, my tasks, upcoming deadlines, GitHub activity, recent feedback. Persistent “Submit Update” & “Raise Concern”.
How
Aggregates the mentee’s own records + team milestones; activity rail pulls from GitHub/Calendar.
Owner
Read-only roll-up. Actions: Mentee submits updates & concerns.
Tasks/mentee/tasks
Why
A clear list of concrete work the mentee is responsible for.
Does
Lists assigned tasks with project, progress, due date, status. The mentee updates their own progress.
How
Filtered to assignee = me. Progress edits are the only writes the mentee has here.
Owner
Created & assigned by Mentor · progress updated by Mentee.
Deliverables/mentee/deliverables
Why
The tangible artifacts that prove the work (report, demo video, code, slides).
Does
Shows required deliverables and their review state (Pending / Approved / Rejected); mentee submits a file/link.
How
Deliverable types are configured by Admin; the mentor requires them per milestone; the mentee uploads.
Owner
Type by Admin · required by Mentor · submitted by Mentee · approved by Mentor.
Milestones/mentee/milestones
Why
So the student always knows the bigger checkpoints, not just today’s task.
Does
Shows the project’s milestones grouped by phase, with key output, progress and due date (read-only for the mentee).
How
Milestones belong to the team’s project; the mentee’s task progress rolls up into milestone completion.
Owner
Set by Mentor · signed off by Teacher · worked toward by Mentee.
Reviews/mentee/reviews
Why
The mentee’s accountability surface — where they report progress and read how they’re being assessed.
Does
Three tabs: Submit Update (the L1 4-field form), My Updates (history), and Weekly Reviews (the mentor’s L3 + teacher’s L4 about them).
How
L1 is the mentee’s own write; L3/L4 are read-only here (written by mentor/teacher).
Owner
L1 by Mentee · L3 by Mentor · L4 by Teacher.
Feedback/mentee/feedback
Why
Two-way accountability: the mentee both reads mentor feedback and rates the mentor (360°).
Does
Shows feedback received, plus a “Rate your mentor” card: was the mentor available? was the feedback useful? + comments.
How
360° responses are aggregated into the mentor’s performance score the teacher/LCC sees.
Owner
Feedback given by Mentor · 360° rating by Mentee.
Team/mentee/team
Why
The student’s sense of belonging and shared context (who’s on my team, our group project, our channel).
Does
Shows team members, the assigned mentor & teacher, the group project, and the Discord channel.
How
Team membership is set by Admin/LCC; the group project is shared across the team.
Owner
Team built by Admin/LCC · led by Mentor.

08Mentor sidebar — every item explained

Mentor is team-scoped and wears two hats: Mentorship (managing ~5 mentees) and Team Delivery (leading the build — because the Student Mentor is the team lead). The Mentor is the biggest creator/assigner of work.

Mentorship

Dashboard/mentor
Why
Triage view — see at a glance which mentees need attention this week.
Does
Counts (mentees, updates this week, at-risk, reviews due) + “mentees needing attention” + pending reviews.
How
Reads L1 updates + auto-flags across the mentor’s assigned mentees.
Owner
Roll-up for Mentor.
My Mentees/mentor/mentees
Why
The core mentor screen — the L2 dashboard that drives the 2-day pulse.
Does
A row per mentee: updates this week, last update, blocker streak, status 🟢🟡🔴, days since update, action needed. Mentor sets L2 status here.
How
Auto-computed from L1 updates; the mentor adds the status + comment every 2 days.
Owner
L2 written by Mentor · source data from Mentee updates.
Reviews/mentor/reviews
Why
Where the mentor produces the weekly evaluation (L3) that the teacher then signs (L4).
Does
One card per mentee: Progress Summary, Strength, Improvement Area, auto-flag, and Mentor Status (On Track / At Risk / Needs Discussion). “Write review”.
How
Due every Saturday; on submit it’s handed to the teacher’s L4 queue.
Owner
L3 by Mentor → reviewed by Teacher.

Team Delivery mentor leads the team

Team/mentor/team
Why
The mentor needs the full roster and team meta to lead delivery.
Does
Team roster (members, roles, status, last active) + repo & channel mapping.
Owner
Membership by Admin/LCC · led by Mentor.
Issues & PRs (Board)/mentor/board
Why
A Jira-style view of the team’s real engineering work, so delivery is visible — not self-reported at the end.
Does
Kanban: Backlog → In Progress → In Review → Done → Released, with cards from GitHub issues/PRs + platform tasks.
How
GitHub integration syncs commits/PRs/issues per team repo via webhooks + nightly pull.
Owner
Issues/PRs by the Mentees (developers) · triaged by Mentor.
Blockers/mentor/blockers
Why
A focused queue of what’s stuck, with SLA timers, so the mentor unblocks fast and escalates on time.
Does
Lists blocked tasks + high-severity concerns with severity, age, and SLA (overdue in red).
How
Pulls blockers from L1 updates + flagged items; SLA thresholds are configurable; stale ones escalate.
Owner
Raised by Mentee · worked/escalated by Mentor → Teacher.
Tasks/mentor/tasks
Why
Where the mentor breaks work down and assigns it — the source of every mentee’s task list.
Does
Lists all team tasks; the mentor creates a task and assigns it to a mentee (title, project, due, milestone).
Owner
Created & assigned by Mentor → done by Mentee.
Deliverables/mentor/deliverables
Why
The mentor is the first reviewer/approver of submitted artifacts.
Does
Lists submitted deliverables; Approve/Reject with feedback on pending ones.
Owner
Submitted by Mentee/team · approved by Mentor · completion tracked by Teacher.

09Teacher sidebar — every item explained

Teacher (Faculty Mentor) is domain-scoped: they see every team, mentor and student in their domain only. They are the approver and final reviewer, not the day-to-day assigner.

Domain Overview/teacher
Why
A bird’s-eye view of the whole domain’s health to spot risk early.
Does
Teams/students counts, on-track vs at-risk, pending L4 reviews, a groups grid, and the faculty gates panel.
Owner
Roll-up across the Teacher’s domain.
Teams/teacher/teams
Why
See how each team (and its mentor) is doing.
Does
Table of all domain teams: mentor, squad, members, completion, status.
Owner
Read across domain · teams built by Admin/LCC.
Students/teacher/students
Why
Drill into individual student progress regardless of team.
Does
All domain mentees with squad, team, mentor, completion, L2 status, GitHub commits.
Owner
Read-only · status data from Mentor.
Reviews & Gates/teacher/reviews
Why
The teacher’s two decision powers: the weekly L4 call and the faculty approval gates.
Does
L4 tab: reads each mentor’s L3 and sets the decision (Continue / Monitor / Schedule Discussion). Gates tab: Approve / Revise & Resubmit / Reject proposals and sign off milestones.
How
L4 is due every Sunday; gate verdicts unlock the next phase.
Owner
L4 & gate verdicts by Teacher · L3 input from Mentor.
Mentor Performance/teacher/mentors
Why
Mentors must stay accountable too — the teacher monitors mentor quality.
Does
Per-mentor: review completion %, mentee success, 360° score, status.
How
Built from L2/L3 on-time rates + the mentees’ 360° feedback.
Owner
Reviewed by Teacher · fed by Mentee 360° + Mentor activity.
Analytics/teacher/analytics
Why
Trends and comparisons to manage the domain, not just react.
Does
Team completion, update compliance by squad, and domain KPIs.
Owner
Read-only analytics for Teacher.

10LCC & Admin — the frame and the oversight

These two roles don’t do day-to-day mentoring; they set up the drive and watch the whole thing.

LCC — coordination & oversight

Global Dashboard / Drive HealthWatch completion, blockers, concerns, inactive teams across all domains.
Domains / TeamsCompare every domain & team; spot laggards.
ConcernsThe triage queue — LCC is the first point of contact; resolve & track SLA.
Email CenterCompose/schedule bulk email targeted by domain/team/role.
OnboardingTrack who joined Discord / activated accounts; chase the rest.
DemeritsRecord warnings/demerits for repeated inactivity; escalate serious cases.
AnalyticsDrive-wide health, domain comparison, engagement.

Admin — system & configuration

UsersCreate/import users (no public signup), assign roles, reset passwords.
Domains / TeamsBuild the org structure; assign mentor↔team↔teacher.
RolesView the RBAC matrix (role × permission).
ConfigurationThe heart of “nothing is hardcoded.” Phases, gates, review cycles, escalation thresholds, rubrics — all editable here.
IntegrationsConnect GitHub / Discord / Google Calendar; map repos & channels.
Email TemplatesEdit the templates that automated emails & concern routing use.
Audit LogsImmutable record of every privileged action.
Why Admin/LCC configure but don’t assign: they define the rules of the game (phases, deadlines, thresholds, rubrics) so the drive can change year to year without code. The actual mentoring work — milestones, tasks, reviews, blockers — is owned by the people closest to the student: the Mentor and the Teacher.
